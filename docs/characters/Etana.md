# Etana

**Etana** was a legendary king of Kiš (Kish) in ancient Sumer, best known from the *Etana Epic*, a text that describes his quest for a "birth plant" or "herb of life" to produce an heir — and his extraordinary flight to heaven on the back of an eagle.

## The Etana Epic

The story of Etana is preserved on fragmentary cuneiform tablets from Nineveh, [Sippar](../places/Sippar.md), and [Uruk](../places/Uruk.md), dating to the Middle Assyrian and Neo-Assyrian periods (c. 13th–7th centuries BCE). The narrative includes:

1. **The Childless King** — Etana, the king of Kish, has no son to inherit his throne and prays to the gods for an heir
2. **The Eagle and the Serpent** — An eagle and a serpent make a pact of friendship, but the eagle betrays and devours the serpent's young
3. **Shamash's Judgment** — The sun god [Shamash](Utu.md) punishes the eagle by trapping it in a pit
4. **Etana Rescues the Eagle** — Guided by Shamash, Etana rescues the eagle from the pit
5. **The Flight to Heaven** — In gratitude, the eagle carries Etana to the heavens in search of the "birth plant"
6. **The Earth Below** — As they ascend, the Earth shrinks beneath them — a description reminiscent of a view from altitude
7. **The Conclusion** — The tablet breaks off, but Etana apparently fails to reach his goal; later tradition gives him a son

## Sitchin's Interpretation

> *"The story of Etana is not a fairy tale. It is an eyewitness account of an ancient flight in a spacecraft — a human who was taken to the heavens by one of the Anunnaki."*

Sitchin argued:

1. **The Eagle** — The "eagle" was not a bird but a flying vehicle (a [SHEM](../concepts/SHEM.md) or rocketship), possibly piloted by a god or guided remotely
2. **The View from Above** — Etana's description of the Earth shrinking below him as he ascended matches the experience of gaining altitude in a spacecraft
3. **The Birth Plant** — The "plant" Etana sought was not a literal herb but a form of genetic treatment or Anunnaki medical technology
4. **Historical Person** — Etana appears in the Sumerian King List as the first king of Kish, ruling after the flood

## Etana in the Sumerian King List

> *"Etana, the shepherd, who ascended to heaven and consolidated all the lands, became king and ruled for 1,560 years."*

The extraordinary length of Etana's reign (along with other antediluvian kings) is interpreted by Sitchin as a reflection of the [NÉRU](../concepts/neru.md) system — 1,560 years being 1,560 Nibiru-years or some calendrical calculation.

## The Iconography

Cylinder seals from Mesopotamia frequently depict the Etana narrative — a man riding a large eagle ascending toward the sky. Sitchin read these as literal depictions of space flight.
