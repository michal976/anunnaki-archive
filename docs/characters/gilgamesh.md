# Gilgamesh

**Gilgameš** (Akkadian: *Gilgamesh*; Sumerian: *Bilgames*) was a legendary king of [Uruk](../places/Uruk.md) and the hero of the *Epic of Gilgamesh*, one of the oldest surviving works of literature (c. 2100 BCE). In Zecharia Sitchin's reading, Gilgamesh was a historical figure — a semi-divine human who sought the secret of immortality from the Anunnaki.

## The Epic of Gilgamesh

The epic poem, inscribed on twelve tablets, tells the story of:

1. **The Tyrant King** — Gilgamesh, two-thirds god and one-third human, oppresses the people of Uruk
2. **Enkidu** — The gods create Enkidu, a wild man, to challenge Gilgamesh
3. **The Friendship** — Gilgamesh and Enkidu become close friends after wrestling
4. **The Cedar Forest** — They journey to kill the monster Humbaba
5. **Ishtar's Rejection** — Gilgamesh rejects the goddess Ishtar ([Inanna](Inanna.md)), enraging her
6. **The Bull of Heaven** — Ishtar sends the Bull of Heaven, which they kill
7. **Enkidu's Death** — The gods decree Enkidu's death as punishment
8. **The Quest for Immortality** — Grieving, Gilgamesh journeys to find Utnapishtim, the survivor of the Great Flood
9. **The Plant of Youth** — Utnapishtim's wife gives Gilgamesh a plant that restores youth
10. **The Serpent Steals the Plant** — A snake steals the plant while Gilgamesh bathes
11. **The Return** — Gilgamesh returns to Uruk, accepting his mortality

## Sitchin's Interpretation

Sitchin devoted significant attention to Gilgamesh in *The Stairway to Heaven*:

> *"The Epic of Gilgamesh is not a myth. It is a historical account of a Sumerian king's failed attempt to obtain the technology of the gods — the secret of immortality — from the Anunnaki."*

Key points of Sitchin's reading:

1. **Gilgamesh as Historical King** — Gilgamesh is listed in the Sumerian King List and appears in other contemporary texts
2. **The Cedar Forest** — The journey to the Cedar Forest was a journey to the [Sinai](../places/sinai.md) spaceport
3. **Utnapishtim the Astronaut** — Utnapishtim was a survivor of the flood who had been granted immortality by the Anunnaki and resided in [Tilmun](../places/Tilmun.md), the land of the rocketships
4. **The Plant of Youth** — The "plant" was not botanical but technological — possibly a medical treatment or genetic therapy
5. **The Serpent** — The snake that steals the plant was a symbol of [Enki](Enki.md) (the serpent god) or an Anunnaki agent preventing Gilgamesh from stealing immortality

## The Quest for Tilmun

Gilgamesh's journey to find Utnapishtim involved:
- Traveling to the "Mashu Mountain" (a twin-peaked mountain, possibly [Ararat](../places/Ararat.md))
- Passing through a tunnel guarded by "scorpion-men" (Anunnaki guards)
- Crossing the "waters of death" (a restricted area)
- Arriving at the island of the blessed [Tilmun](../places/Tilmun.md)

## Cuneiform Evidence

The name **Gilgameš** (Sumerian: *Bilgames*) is attested in Sumerian and Akkadian cuneiform texts from the third millennium BCE. The Epic of Gilgamesh is one of the oldest surviving works of world literature.

- **[CDLI Corpus: Gilgamesh](https://cdli.earth/search?q=Gilgamesh)** — Browse tablets mentioning Gilgamesh
- **Key tablet:** The *Standard Babylonian Epic of Gilgamesh* (CDLI P354853) — A twelve-tablet version from the library of Ashurbanipal at Nineveh (7th century BCE). The epic includes the flood narrative and Gilgamesh's quest for immortality. Older Sumerian tales about Bilgames also survive on tablets from Nippur and Ur.
