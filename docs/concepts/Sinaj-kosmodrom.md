# Sinajský kosmodrom (Sinai Spaceport)

The **Sinai Spaceport** was, according to Zecharia Sitchin, the primary landing and take-off facility for the [[Anunnaki]] on Earth. Located in the [[Sinaj|Sinai Peninsula]], it was the central hub of the Anunnaki space transportation system.

## The Location

Sitchin identified the Sinai spaceport on the central plateau of the Sinai Peninsula, specifically:

- The area around the mountain known to Egyptians as the "Stairway of the Gods"
- The region near what became St. Catherine's Monastery
- The flat, elevated plateau suitable for spacecraft landings

## The Landing Corridor

The spaceport was part of a precisely aligned corridor:

| Landmark | Role |
|----------|------|
| [[Ararat]] | Northern approach landmark |
| [[Jeruzalém]] | Mount Moriah — guidance waypoint |
| [[Baalbek]] | Launch platform |
| [[Gíza]] | Primary navigation beacon |
| [[Sinaj]] | Main spaceport landing field |

## The "Stairway of the Gods"

The Egyptian name for the Sinai mountains — *Ta-ur* or "the stairway" — preserved the memory of the spaceport's function. The biblical "ladder" in Jacob's dream and the Sumerian [[DUR.AN.KI]] all referred to the same thing: the connection point between Earth and the heavens.
