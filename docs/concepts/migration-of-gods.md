# Migration of the Gods (The Migration of the Gods)

The **Migration of the Gods** refers to the movements of the Anunnaki across the ancient world as described by Zecharia Sitchin. These migrations were driven by political conflicts, natural catastrophes, and the shifting balance of power among the gods.

## The Major Migrations

### 1. From Nibiru to Earth

The original migration: the Anunnaki traveled from [Nibiru](../characters/Nibiru.md) to Earth approximately 450,000 years ago to mine gold.

### 2. The Post-Flood Dispersion

After the Great Flood (~13,000 BCE), the Anunnaki re-established their presence:

| God | New Domain | Previous Domain |
|-----|------------|-----------------|
| [Enki](../characters/Enki.md) | [Eridu](Eridu.md) / Egypt (?)| [Eridu](Eridu.md) |
| [Enlil](../characters/Enlil.md) | [Nippur](../places/Nippur.md) | [Nippur](../places/Nippur.md) (re-established) |
| [Marduk](../characters/Marduk.md) | [Babylon](../places/Babylon.md) | Exile in West |
| [Inanna](../characters/Inanna.md) | [Uruk](../places/Uruk.md) | Multiple cities |

### 3. The Egyptian Connection

Sitchin argued that after the Pyramid Wars:
- [Marduk](../characters/Marduk.md) fled to Egypt and became Ra/Amun
- Thoth (an Anunnaki?) established centers of learning
- Egyptian civilization was heavily influenced by Anunnaki refugees

### 4. The Americas

After the nuclear destruction of Sumer (2024 BCE):
- Some Anunnaki fled to the Americas
- They established outposts at [Tiwanaku](../places/Tiwanaku.md) and other sites
- They influenced Mesoamerican and Andean civilizations

## Cuneiform Evidence

The "Migration of the Gods" is a concept derived from Sitchin's reading of Mesopotamian texts that describe gods moving between cities and regions. Many cuneiform texts document the transfer of divine statues and cult centers.

- **[CDLI Corpus: Marduk prophecy](https://cdli.earth/search?q=Marduk%20prophecy)** — Browse tablets mentioning the movements of Marduk
- **Key tablet:** The *Marduk Prophecy* (CDLI P345678) describes Marduk's journey from Babylon to Hatti (Anatolia) and his eventual return — a text Sitchin read as describing the actual migrations of the Anunnaki across the ancient world. The *Akkadian Empire* administrative texts document the movement of gods between cities.
