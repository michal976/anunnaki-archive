# Friedrich — Linguistic Critique

The linguistic critique of Zecharia Sitchin's work focuses on his treatment of Sumerian, Akkadian, and Hebrew languages.

## Key Issues

1. **Sumerian is not Semitic** — Sitchin treated Sumerian as if it shared roots with Semitic languages (Hebrew, Akkadian)
2. **Etymological Fallacies** — Sitchin connected words based on superficial similarities
3. **Hebrew "Shem"** — The word *shem* in Hebrew means "name," not "rocketship"
4. **Misreading of Cuneiform** — Sitchin read signs in ways not supported by established lexicography
