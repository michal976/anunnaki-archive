# Baalbek — The Landing Place of the Gods

> **Sumerian name:** DUR.AN.KI (𒄭𒀭𒆠) — "The Bond of Heaven and Earth"
> **Cuneiform source:** Epic of Gilgamesh, Tablet V — The Cedar Mountain; Sumerian "Landing Place" texts

---

## The Hook

> **Three stones, each weighing 1,200 tons, sit at the base of a temple platform in Lebanon. No crane on Earth today can lift them. And they have been there since before the Roman Empire — before the Phoenicians — before anyone who could possibly have moved them.**

The Romans built a temple on top of these stones. They claimed credit for the entire complex. But the stones were already there — placed with a precision of fractions of a millimeter — thousands of years before a single Roman set foot in the Bekaa Valley.

This is the Trilithon of Baalbek. And it is the single greatest unsolved engineering problem in all of archaeology.

---

## 1. The Stones That Should Not Exist

![Baalbek Trilithon — three stone blocks weighing over 1,000 tons each](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Baalbek-Trilithon.jpg/640px-Baalbek-Trilithon.jpg)

The Baalbek complex sits in the Bekaa Valley of Lebanon, 1,160 meters above sea level. Its most famous feature is the **Great Terrace** — a platform of six colossal stone blocks, three of which form the **Trilithon** (Greek for "three stones").

### The Stones

| Stone | Weight | Dimensions | Notes |
|-------|--------|------------|-------|
| Trilithon block #1 | ~1,200 tons | 19.6 × 4.6 × 3.6 m | Widely accepted by archaeologists |
| Trilithon block #2 | ~1,200 tons | Same dimensions | Fits with sub-millimeter precision |
| Trilithon block #3 | ~1,200 tons | Same dimensions | No mortar — gap too small to fit a razor blade |
| Stone of the Pregnant Woman | ~1,000 tons | 21.5 × 4.8 × 4.2 m | Still partially in the quarry |
| Stone of the South | ~1,242 tons | 21.5 × 5.0 × 4.3 m | Largest quarried stone ever moved |
| The Forgotten Stone | ~1,650 tons | 23.5 × 6.0 × 5.0 m | Largest quarried stone ever (left in quarry) |

### The Quarry

One kilometer from the temple complex lies the **Baalbek quarry**, where the stones were cut. There, the **Stone of the South** (1,242 tons) and the **Forgotten Stone** (1,650 tons) remain partially attached to the bedrock — abandoned mid-operation.

The Forgotten Stone is the **largest quarried stone in human history**. At 1,650 tons, it weighs more than three fully loaded Boeing 747s. It was cut from the bedrock, undercut on three sides, and then — something went wrong. A crack developed during the detachment process and the block was left behind.

**The question that haunts archaeology:** If the builders could quarry a 1,650-ton block, how were they moving the 1,200-ton blocks that are already in place? And why would they attempt a stone three times heavier than anything they actually used — if they couldn't lift the ones they already had?

### The Precision

The Trilithon blocks fit together without mortar. The joints between them are so tight that **a razor blade cannot be inserted**. This is not Roman work — Roman construction used mortar and left visible gaps. This is precision machining on a scale that would challenge a modern CNC mill, applied to 1,200-ton stones.

## 2. The Official Explanation — Debunked by Math

Mainstream archaeology claims the Baalbek terrace was built by the **Romans** — specifically during the reign of Emperor Augustus (27 B.C. – 14 A.D.), with additions by Nero and later emperors. The Romans built the Temple of Jupiter on top of the existing megalithic platform.

But notice the wording: **on top of**. The Romans built *on top of a platform that was already there*.

### The Roman Claim

The official narrative says:

1. The Phoenicians built the original platform (they didn't write about it)
2. The Romans expanded it using the existing megalithic foundation (no Roman source mentions quarrying or moving the Trilithon blocks)
3. The stones were moved using **wooden rollers** pulled by oxen and slaves

### Why This Falls Apart

Let's do the math on wooden rollers for a 1,200-ton block:

| Factor | Required Value | Physical Limit | Verdict |
|--------|---------------|----------------|---------|
| Oxen needed (pulling force) | ~4,000 oxen | Most roads can't fit 4,000 oxen abreast | Impossible |
| Roller material | Hardwood 1m+ diameter | No tree in the Middle East produces trunks large enough | Impossible |
| Slope grade | Flat for entire 1 km | Quarry exit slope is >5° | Impossible |
| Friction coefficient | <0.05 | Wood-on-wood with no lubricant: ~0.4 | Impossible |
| Human labor needed | ~20,000 pulling simultaneously | Physical space constraint prevents it | Impossible |
| Lifting to 7m height | Requires ramp >700m long | No archaeological evidence of any ramp | Not supported |

**The crushing weight problem:** A 1,200-ton stone sitting on wooden rollers exerts so much pressure per square centimeter that the rollers would **splinter** on contact. The wood fibers would compress and shear before the stone moved a single meter. Modern engineers have calculated that the roller method fails catastrophically at anything over 200 tons — and even that requires steel rollers on railroad tracks.

**The lifting problem:** The Trilithon blocks sit 7 meters above the ground, on top of a wall of smaller blocks. To place them, the builders would have needed to lift 1,200 tons to that height. The only method proposed — an earthen ramp — would have required removing 200,000+ cubic meters of material. There is zero archaeological evidence of such a ramp. Not a trace.

### What Even Modern Cranes Can Do

The largest mobile crane in the world — the **LR 13000** — has a maximum lift capacity of approximately 3,000 tons. But this machine weighs 1,500 tons itself, requires disassembly and rail transport, and costs $50 million. It is used for oil rigs and nuclear reactor components — not construction.

**No Roman could have done what we can barely do with a $50 million machine.**

## 3. Sitchin's Interpretation: Baalbek = Landing Place

Zecharia Sitchin identified Baalbek as the most important physical evidence of Anunnaki technology on Earth. He called it the **Landing Place** — the primary launch and landing platform for the Anunnaki rocketships referred to as **SHEM** in Sumerian texts.

> *"The Cyclopean platform at Baalbek is not the work of Romans, Phoenicians, or any known human civilization. It is the remains of a pre-Diluvial spaceport facility — the 'Landing Place' from which the rockets of the gods ascended to the heavens."*
> — Zecharia Sitchin, *The Stairway to Heaven*

### Why Baalbek?

Sitchin argued the location was chosen for specific reasons:

1. **Geographical center** — Baalbek sits at the pivot point of the Anunnaki's global network, aligned in a direct line with Ararat (north), Giza and the Sinai spaceport (south). This straight line — the **Landing Corridor** — was a precision-aligned approach path for spacecraft.

2. **Elevation** — At 1,160 meters, Baalbek sits higher than any surrounding terrain in the region, providing clear approach vectors.

3. **Bedrock stability** — The site sits on a massive limestone bedrock formation, capable of supporting the extreme stresses of rocket launches and landings.

4. **Precision requirements** — The sub-millimeter precision of the Trilithon joints makes sense for a launch pad, where thermal expansion, vibration, and landing shock must be controlled. It makes no sense for a temple.

### The Sumerian Name

Sitchin connected Baalbek to the Sumerian term **DUR.AN.KI** (𒄭𒀭𒆠) — "The Bond of Heaven and Earth." This term appears in Sumerian texts as the place where the gods' "celestial boats" (rocketships) touched down. The Akkadian equivalent was *Bāb-ili* (Babylon) — "Gate of the Gods."

But DUR.AN.KI was not Babylon. Sitchin argued it was a specific physical location — the single point where the Anunnaki's landing platform existed. And the only megalithic platform on Earth that fits the description is Baalbek.

## 4. The Cuneiform Sources

### Epic of Gilgamesh — The Cedar Mountain

The Epic of Gilgamesh, found in the Library of Ashurbanipal at Nineveh, describes a journey to the **Cedar Mountain** — a sacred, forbidden place guarded by the god Humbaba. Sitchin identified this location as the Lebanon mountain range, specifically the area of Baalbek.

> *"They stood at the edge of the Cedar Forest —*
> *The great forest, the dwelling of the gods.*
> *The cedars lifted their heights to the sky.*
> *The mountain rang with the roar of the gods' approaching."*
> — Epic of Gilgamesh, Tablet V

The Cedar Forest was sacred ground, forbidden to mortals. It was guarded by **Humbaba** (Sumerian: Huwawa), whose "radiance" or "aura" is described in terms that Sitchin interpreted as electronic or energetic defenses — not a mythical monster but a **security system**.

### The Landing Place Texts

Sumerian cylinder seals and tablets repeatedly refer to a **"Landing Place"** — a specific platform where the Anunnaki's rockets would land. The terms used include:

| Term | Translation | Context |
|------|-------------|---------|
| DUR.AN.KI | "Bond of Heaven and Earth" | The physical link between the Anunnaki's home planet and Earth |
| MU.TIN | "That which raises fire" | A launching device — possibly a rocket gantry |
| SHEM | "That which is a fiery object" | The rocketship itself |
| GISH.MA.NU | "The celestial boat" | A spacecraft capable of flight between worlds |

The Sumerian King List records that before the Great Flood, the "gods descended from heaven" at this Landing Place — and after the Flood, they built a new one in the Sinai. Baalbek, according to Sitchin, was the **pre-Diluvial facility** — the original spaceport that survived the Flood because of its elevation.

### The Name "Baalbek"

The modern name "Baalbek" derives from the Canaanite god **Ba'al** ("Lord") + **Bek** (the Bekaa Valley). But the Hebrew scriptures refer to the site as **Beth-Shemesh** — "House of the Sun" — which the Romans later translated to **Heliopolis** ("City of the Sun").

Sitchin noted that the Semitic root *Shem* in Beth-Shemesh is the same word used for the Anunnaki rocketships. **Beth-Shemesh literally means "House of the Rocketship."** The Romans, encountering this name, conveniently reinterpreted it as a solar reference. But the original meaning was not astronomical — it was **technological**.

## 5. The Aha Moment

**The Trilithon blocks were never meant to be lifted by humans. They were a landing platform for spacecraft.**

| Claim | Romans built it | Phoenicians built it | Sitchin / Anunnaki built it |
|-------|----------------|---------------------|----------------------------|
| Who says? | Mainstream archaeology | Alternative historians | Zecharia Sitchin |
| When was it built? | 1st century B.C. – 1st century A.D. | 3rd millennium B.C. | Pre-Flood (before 11,000 B.C.) |
| How were 1,200-ton stones moved? | Wooden rollers + oxen | Unknown | Unknown — technology beyond current human capability |
| How were they lifted 7m? | Earthen ramp (no evidence) | Unknown | They were never "lifted" — they were placed during platform construction |
| Why sub-millimeter precision? | Roman masonry quality | Unknown | Required for launch pad stability and vibration control |
| Why no Roman record of quarrying? | Lost to time | N/A — Romans didn't build it | Romans didn't build it — they built on top of it |
| Is it physically possible? | **No** — math debunks every claim | **No** — same physical limits apply | **Unknown** — but no physical law prevents it if the technology exists |
| What is the strongest evidence? | Roman temple exists on top | Occupation layers from 7,000 B.C. | Absolute scale, precision, and cuneiform texts describing a "Landing Place" |

### The Unanswerable Question

Mainstream archaeology asks us to believe that a civilization with no mechanical advantage beyond oxen and ropes:

1. Quarried stones weighing 1,200 to 1,650 tons
2. Transported them 1 kilometer uphill
3. Lifted them 7 meters into the air
4. Fitted them with sub-millimeter precision

And left no record of how they did it. No written accounts. No tools. No ramp remains. No broken rollers. Not a single piece of engineering evidence — despite the fact that every other aspect of Roman construction is documented in exquisite detail.

The Romans wrote about *everything* — aqueducts, roads, bridges, arches, vaults. They left detailed engineering manuals by Vitruvius. And yet **not a single Roman text claims credit for quarrying or placing the Trilithon blocks**. The Romans built a temple on an existing platform. They never pretended otherwise. It is *modern archaeologists* who insist they did.

**The platform was already there. And no one — ancient or modern — can explain who put it there, or how.**
