# Gnosticismus (Gnosticism)

Gnosticism was a religious movement in the early Christian era that emphasized secret knowledge (*gnosis*) as the path to salvation. Some parallels between Gnostic teachings and Sitchin's [[Anunnaki]] narrative are noteworthy.

## Gnostic Parallels

| Gnostic Concept | Anunnaki Parallel |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| The Demiurge (lesser creator god) | [[Enlil]] — the stern commander |
| The Archons (rulers of the material world) | The [[Anunnaki]] |
| Secret knowledge saved from the creator | The [[ME]] — hidden wisdom |
| The "alien God" beyond the Demiurge | [[Anu]] — the distant king of Nibiru |
| The serpent as a giver of knowledge | [[Enki]] — the wise serpent god |

## Sitchin's View

Sitchin noted that Gnostics preserved the idea that the creator of the material world (the Demiurge) was not the supreme God — a concept that aligns with his reading of [[Enlil]] as a lesser (though powerful) being compared to [[Anu]].
