# Mezoamerika (Mesoamerican Religions)

Zecharia Sitchin devoted significant attention to [[Mezoamerika|Mesoamerican]] civilizations in his book *The Lost Realms*. He argued that the Olmecs, Mayans, and Aztecs preserved traditions brought by [[Marduk]] and other Anunnaki refugees.

## Key Parallels

| Mesoamerican Figure | Anunnaki Connection |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| [[Quetzalcoatl]] | The feathered serpent — [[Enki]] |
| [[Tezcatlipoca]] | A rival god — [[Enlil]] |
| [[Kukulkan]] | Mayan version of Quetzalcoatl |
| [[Viracocha]] | The creator god of the Andes |

## The Calendars

The Mayan Long Count calendar, with its cycles of 5,125 and 26,000 years, was seen by Sitchin as a version of the Anunnaki timekeeping system.
