# Sumer — The Original Religion

Sumerian religion was the original source from which all later Near Eastern religions derived, according to Zecharia Sitchin. The [[Anunnaki]] pantheon of Sumer was not a mythological invention but a historical record of real beings.

## The Pantheon

The Sumerian pantheon consisted of the great [[Anunnaki]] — gods who lived in cities, demanded worship and offerings, and directly intervened in human affairs. The chief gods were:

- [[Anu]] — Sky god, king of [[Nibiru]]
- [[Enlil]] — Lord of the wind, commander of Earth
- [[Enki]] — Lord of the waters, creator of humanity
- [[Ninmah]] — Mother goddess, geneticist
- [[Inanna]] — Goddess of love and war
- [[Utu]] — Sun god, spaceport commander

## Sumerian Religious Practices

- Temples (*é*) as the homes of the gods
- Ziggurats as artificial mountains connecting heaven and Earth
- The sacred marriage ritual
- Divination and astrology
- The New Year festival (*Akitu*)

## Sitchin's View

Sitchin argued that Sumerian religion was not "religion" in the modern sense — it was the literal administration of Anunnaki rule over humanity.
