# Řecko (Greek Religion)

The mythology of ancient [Greece](greece.md) shows clear parallels with the Mesopotamian and Anunnaki narratives, according to Zecharia Sitchin.

## Parallels

| Greek God | Anunnaki Counterpart |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Zeus | [Enlil](../characters/Enlil.md) — sky god, ruler |
| Poseidon | [Enki](../characters/Enki.md) — god of the waters |
| Hades | [Nergal](../characters/Nergal.md) — god of the underworld |
| Aphrodite | [Inanna](../characters/Inanna.md) — goddess of love |
| Ares | [Ninurta](../characters/Ninurta.md) — god of war |
| Hermes | [Nabu](../characters/Nabu.md) — messenger god |
| Prometheus | [Enki](../characters/Enki.md) — creator and benefactor of humans |
| Atlas | The Anunnaki who held up the sky |

## The Titans

The Greek myth of the Titans — the older generation of gods overthrown by the Olympians — parallels Sitchin's narrative of the older Anunnaki generation ([Anu](../characters/Anu.md), [Enlil](../characters/Enlil.md), [Enki](../characters/Enki.md)) being challenged by the younger generation ([Marduk](../characters/Marduk.md), [Inanna](../characters/Inanna.md)).
