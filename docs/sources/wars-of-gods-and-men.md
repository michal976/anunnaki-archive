# The Wars of Gods and Men (Války bohů a lidí)

*The Wars of Gods and Men*, published in 1985, is the third book in Zecharia Sitchin's Earth Chronicles series. It details the conflicts among the Anunnaki themselves and the wars that shaped the ancient Near East.

## Overview

The book spans from the earliest conflicts in the heavens (the celestial battle between [Nibiru](../characters/Nibiru.md) and Tiamat) to the final nuclear destruction of [Sumer](../religions/Sumer.md) in 2024 BCE. Key chapters include:

1. **The Celestial Battle** — The war in heaven
2. **The Pyramid Wars** — The conflict over control of the spaceport
3. **The Patriarchs** — The biblical patriarchs as participants in Anunnaki politics
4. **The Kingship** — The institution of kingship as an Anunnaki invention
5. **The Exiles** — The forced migrations of peoples
6. **The Nuclear Holocaust** — The final destruction of Sumer

## The Pyramid Wars

The central conflict in the book is the series of confrontations known as the [Pyramid Wars](../timeline/pyramid-wars.md) — the struggle between the clans of [Enki](../characters/Enki.md) and [Enlil](../characters/Enlil.md) over control of the spaceport facilities:

- **Enlil's faction** — Wished to maintain strict control over human civilization
- **Enki's faction** — Favored granting humanity more knowledge and autonomy
- **Marduk's ambitions** — [Marduk](../characters/Marduk.md), Enki's firstborn son, sought supremacy over all gods
- **The outcome** — The spaceport at [Sippar](../places/Sippar.md) was destroyed in a nuclear strike

## The Nuclear Event

Sitchin identifies 2024 BCE as the year of the nuclear destruction of Sumer. He cites:

- Archaeological evidence of "leveled" cities at [Ur](../places/Ur.md), [Nippur](../places/Nippur.md), and [Uruk](../places/Uruk.md)
- High levels of radiation found in soil layers corresponding to this period
- The biblical account of the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah
- Sumerian lamentations describing "a cloud of death" and "poison in the rivers"

> *"The evil wind, the terrible storm, came upon the land. The cities were destroyed; the people were turned to stone."* — From the Lamentation over the Destruction of Ur

## The Role of the Patriarchs

Sitchin argues that the biblical patriarchs — Abraham, Isaac, Jacob — were not mere religious figures but were directly involved in the Anunnaki power struggles. Abraham's journey from [Ur](../places/Ur.md) to Harran to Canaan was, in Sitchin's reading, a relocation ordered by [Enlil](../characters/Enlil.md) or Yahweh.
