# Mesopotamian Civilization

**Date: 3,800–2,024 BCE**

The flowering of Sumerian civilization was, in Sitchin's view, directly supervised by the Anunnaki. Every aspect of Sumerian culture — writing, law, mathematics, astronomy, agriculture — came from the gods.

## The Sumerian City-States

Each major city was controlled by a patron Anunnaki deity:
- [Eridu](../concepts/Eridu.md) — [Enki](../characters/Enki.md)
- [Nippur](../places/Nippur.md) — [Enlil](../characters/Enlil.md)
- [Ur](../places/Ur.md) — [Nannar](../characters/Nannar.md)
- [Uruk](../places/Uruk.md) — [Inanna](../characters/Inanna.md)
- [Sippar](../places/Sippar.md) — [Utu](../characters/Utu.md)
- [Babylon](../places/Babylon.md) — [Marduk](../characters/Marduk.md)

## The Golden Age

This was the "golden age" of human civilization, when gods and humans lived together. The gods walked among people, gave judgment in their temples, and participated in human affairs.

## The Conflicts

However, this period also saw the beginning of conflicts among the Anunnaki — the [Pyramid Wars](pyramid-wars.md) — as [Marduk](../characters/Marduk.md) sought supremacy and challenged [Enlil](../characters/Enlil.md)'s authority.
